AAZADI KA AMRIT MAHOTSAV

Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav is a series of events to be organized by the Government of India to commemorate the 75th Anniversary of India's Independence. The Mahotsav will be celebrated as a JanUtsav in the spirit of Jan-Bhagidari. The Mahotsav was inaugurated by our Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi on 12th March 2021 and will continue till 15th August 2023. The Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav is an intensive, countrywide campaign that focuses on citizen participation, to be converted into a Janandolan', where small changes, at the local level, will add up to significant national gains. One of the main motives of the Amrit Mahotsav is that every citizen of India should know about all the untold incidents of the freedom struggle. The activities at the school level include Essay competitions, Seminars, Cycle rallies, quizzes, story writing competitions etc.,

INDIA @75YEARS

The sacrifices of our brave Indian freedom fighters are remembered on Independence Day. Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, unfurled the tricolour national flag at Red Fort and Lahori Gate in Delhi when the country became Independent. From that day, India celebrates Independence Day on this day. Many programmes dedicated at Red fort, such as 21 gunshots fired for the start of events, march past by our Indian soldiers, and cultural activities by school kids are held in honour of the soldiers who fought for the nation's independence. Kite flying is also a part of the Independence Day of India's celebration, with kites of various sizes, shapes, and colours filling the sky. From 1974 onwards, the chief ministers of several states unfurled the national flag.

ATMANIRBHAR BHARAT

The year 2020 has been a disastrous one not just for India but for every country in the world due to the rapid spread of coronavirus. which eventually led to a pandemic. The disease impacted the country's economy on a large scale, thus further damaging growth and progress. In these times, when the economy seemed to fall with each passing day, Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi introduced a concept called Atma Nirbhar Bharat. On 12th May 2020, prime Minister Narendra Modi announced a package of 20 Lakhs crore to revive the economy and address the issues affecting various sections of people. The package is about 10% India total GDP. Atma nirbhar Bharat is the mission and vision of our P.M. Narendra Modi to make India Self- reliant. The real meaning of Atma Nirbhar is Self-Reliant India. Atmanirbhar means to promote and give preference to indigenous products match their imported counter parts in cost and quality. Self-reliant doesn't mean that we are court off from the Global world, but it actually means that we are able to produce the products and eventually play a larger role in the global economy.
